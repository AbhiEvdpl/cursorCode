Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about how our pharmacy works with your loved one's care facility.

In a long-term care setting, our pharmacy manages refills directly with your facility's nursing staff and providers. Here's how the process works in Idaho LTC care:

  • Nursing staff monitor medication supplies and request refills before a resident runs low.
  • Our pharmacy verifies the refill authorization and insurance coverage.
  • If a prescription requires provider approval, we contact the prescriber on behalf of the resident.
  • Once approved, the refill is prepared and delivered on the facility's next scheduled delivery day—or sooner if needed.

Families typically do not need to request refills, as the facility and pharmacy coordinate all medication needs automatically.

Insurance changes are common, especially during annual Medicare Part D enrollment or Medicaid updates. When a resident's insurance changes:

  • The facility notifies our pharmacy with the new insurance card or plan information.
  • We update the resident's profile and run coverage checks on all active medications.
  • If any medications require prior authorization or a preferred formulary alternative, we work directly with the prescriber and facility to resolve it.

Families may be contacted if:

  • A new plan has higher copays
  • A medication is not covered
  • Additional financial documentation is needed for Idaho Medicaid

Our billing team ensures a smooth transition with minimal interruption to medication therapy.

For any billing-related questions—copays, statements, insurance coverage, or account changes—families can contact our Billing Department directly.

We provide dedicated support for:

  • Medicare Part D coverage questions
  • Idaho Medicaid billing
  • Private insurance issues
  • Copay clarification
  • Coordination of benefits

Long-term care facilities often need medications urgently, and our Idaho LTC pharmacy provides after-hour delivery services to ensure resident safety.

Here's how emergency and STAT delivery works:

  • A nurse contacts our on-call pharmacist for an urgent medication need.
  • We verify the order from the prescriber and prepare the medication immediately.
  • Emergency deliveries are dispatched through our STAT courier or on-call delivery team.
  • After-hours deliveries are prioritized based on clinical urgency.

Common reasons for emergency delivery include:

  • New admissions
  • New antibiotic starts
  • Pain or symptom-management needs
  • Critical dose changes

Our goal is to ensure facilities receive urgent medications as quickly and safely as possible—day or night.
